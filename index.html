Hello, I’m Henry Kaufman.
I have a knack for creating products.

Growing up, I always had a passion for building stuff. As a young kid, I’d fantasize over the latest LEGO. When I wasn’t building things out of LEGOs, I would be building out of other materials such as cardboard. No matter what, I was always making. My family would go to the Children’s Museum in Denver and I would spend the entire time build various things in the maker section. As I grew older, I built my first computer, and learned the power of technology and the internet. In seventh grade, I was introduced to Minecraft and quickly became addicted. I could build whatever I want, whenever I wanted. Soon after, my friend introduced me to coding, and the development of iOS applications.

Soon enough, I started building my own mini projects on the weekends and in spare time. I would build mini sites that displayed various information. I made a site about school. I made a site for every family vacation I went on. This hobby of mine continued to expand, and I quickly realized that many people do this for a living. I looked into freelancing, but was nervous about the current competition. Nonetheless, I decided to put myself out there, but had little to no success.

High school was where everything began to take off. During my freshman year, I joined TSA (Technology Student Association), where I took my coding skills to a competition. This coding competition proved itself as a very valuable learning experience for myself. We did not win that year (not even come close), but I learned that there were other students out there doing this same thing. Before this, I felt like I was the only person my age doing this—I was wrong.

As sophomore year began again, I started TSA with a passion to win. Our team ended up winning 3rd place at the state competition, giving us a seat at the national competition in June. During my sophomore year of high school, I was able to connect with a fellow young entrepreneur in Denver named Carter. Carter was a 25 year old who was interested in building cool products together. This matched my interest. Nervous about meeting this person by myself, I brought my best friend Jake (a fellow developer), along. Carter introduced us to Greg, the CEO of Canadian Home Buyers Academy, where we began to work on building a platform for customers to interact with their mortgages. We continued this adventure and began to work with other companies like nexleader and LeadLogica.

Over the summer of 2016 I was highly encouraged by my parents to find an internship with a “real company.” After some searching in a few Slack channels, I ended up at Barbershop, a creative technology studio based out of Denver, as a Creative Technology Intern. I had a blast, and was able to learn incredible team skills that I would have never learned just solo freelancing. I learned how to work better with others, and understand transparency between teammates. At the end of the summer I launched a site to explore posts on Luncher, a gamified social platform for people to share their lunches on.

In the fall after school started, I began to work on other side projects with other friends and release more products. A recent project I worked on was Disrupt Cards, a card game designed to make the world a better place through crude humor aimed at the startup community. I teamed up with another friend Nikhil, and we launched a job board for marketing professionals and a site curating the best company documentation pages.

Recently, Canadian Home Buyers Academy has now pivoted themselves into Blink Mortgage, with the idea in mind of helping people find mortgages, quickly, easily, and painlessly. In the coming months, we plan on launching a totally redesigned version of the application, with the customer experience in mind. I’ve also teamed up with the team at Galvanize and am currently helping them build a platform to help students find internships.

Anyways, this is where I am at now. I believe this is just the start.

Let’s begin.
